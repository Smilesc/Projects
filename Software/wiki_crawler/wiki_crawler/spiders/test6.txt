<p><b>Chobani</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Dairy" title="Dairy">dairy</a> <a href="/wiki/Food_processing" title="Food processing">processor</a> specializing in <a href="/wiki/Strained_yogurt" title="Strained yogurt">strained yogurt</a>. The company was founded in 2005 when <a href="/wiki/Hamdi_Ulukaya" title="Hamdi Ulukaya">Hamdi Ulukaya</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> bought a plant in the town of <a href="/wiki/South_Edmeston,_New_York" title="South Edmeston, New York">South Edmeston, New York</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> that was being closed by <a href="/wiki/Kraft_Foods" title="Kraft Foods">Kraft Foods</a>. Ulukaya hired several of the former Kraft employees as well as a "yogurt master" and launched his brand in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-cnn_interview_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnn_interview-5">[5]</a></sup> Chobani sells thick, Greek-style yogurt with a higher protein content than traditional yogurt and is among one of the main companies to popularize this style of yogurt.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The company promotes its products as <a href="/wiki/Health_food" title="Health food">health food</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-science_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-science-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chobani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chobani was founded in 2005 by Hamdi Ulukaya. He chose the name <i>Chobani</i> as a variation of the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a> word <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%C3%A7oban#Turkish" class="extiw" title="wikt:çoban">çoban</a>,</i> itself derived from <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> <i>čupân</i> <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%DA%86%D9%88%D9%BE%D8%A7%D9%86#Persian" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:چوپان">چوپان</a>meaning "shepherd." <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, after seeing an ad for a former <a href="/wiki/Kraft_Foods" title="Kraft Foods">Kraft Foods</a> yogurt plant for sale in <a href="/wiki/South_Edmeston" class="mw-redirect" title="South Edmeston">South Edmeston</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, Ulukaya bought it with a <a href="/wiki/Small_Business_Administration" title="Small Business Administration">Small Business Administration Loan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He launched the strained yogurt business with the help of a handful of the plant's former employees. His goal was to provide Americans with a more authentic, nutritious and accessible yogurt.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In less than five years after launch, Chobani realized over $1 billion in annual sales and became the leading seller of Greek yogurt in America.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2011, Chobani expanded into <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> through the purchase of <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victorian</a> dairy company Bead Foods. It then invested $30m to expand the facility and began manufacturing from the <a href="/wiki/Dandenong_South,_Victoria" title="Dandenong South, Victoria">Dandenong South</a> plant in December 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Chobani became an official sponsor of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="United States at the 2012 Summer Olympics">US Olympic Team</a><sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-16">[16]</a></sup> and premiered their first national commercial during the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics_opening_ceremony" title="2012 Summer Olympics opening ceremony">2012 Summer Olympics opening ceremony</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-syracuse.com_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-syracuse.com-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012 the brand opened its second plant in what became the world’s largest yogurt facility in <a href="/wiki/Twin_Falls,_Idaho" title="Twin Falls, Idaho">Twin Falls, Idaho</a>, with an initial investment of $750 million.  The move created approximately 7,000 jobs, and unemployment rates in the region dropped from 6.3% to 2.4%.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>Chobani also opened its first brick-and-mortar cafe in <a href="/wiki/SoHo,_Manhattan" title="SoHo, Manhattan">SoHo</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">NYC</a> in July 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p>The company temporarily imported its product into Canada in 2011, and briefly sold its products in the U.K. from 2012-2013.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>in 2013 Chobani was ordered to change their yogurt's labeling in England and Wales after a judge ruled that the wording "Greek yoghurt" (rather than Greek-style) misled consumers into thinking the yogurt was made in Greece (yogurt imported from Greece is sold in Britain, and is the only type sold as "Greek").<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 3, 2013, Chobani pulled some of its Greek-style yogurt from supermarket shelves after hearing of "swelling or bloating" in cups. The company said it has investigated and found a type of mold commonly found in dairy that may be to blame.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Chobani announced a voluntary recall on September 5 in cooperation with the <a href="/wiki/FDA" class="mw-redirect" title="FDA">FDA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On September 9, 2013, the FDA reported at least 89 people have reported getting sick after eating the yogurt.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Twin Falls plant won the 2013 DBIA Design-Build Project of the Year award and the 2013 Food Plant of the Year award from <i>Food Engineering</i> magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2016, CEO Hamdi Ulukaya announced that he would be giving Chobani employees 10% of the company's shares.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In October 2016 the company announced it was implementing a program to give six weeks of paid <a href="/wiki/Parental_leave" title="Parental leave">parental leave</a> to new parents.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p><p>Chobani introduced its products into Mexico in August 2016. <sup id="cite_ref-:0_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Chobani surpassed Yoplait as the No. 2 manufacturer in overall yogurt.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> That same year, the company introduced new packaging and a new brand identity as a “food focused wellness company.” <sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The company also announced a $20m expansion of the Twin Falls plant, with a 70,000 square-foot sustainable facility and a new global research and development center. <sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup>
</p><p>When Chobani was launched, Greek yogurt made up less than 1% of the yogurt market in the US. As of 2017, Greek yogurt accounted for 50% of U.S. yogurt sales.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the spring of 2018, CEO Hamdi Ulukaya appeared on the <a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">Ellen Degeneres Show</a><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a> to announce that the company was celebrating its 10th anniversary as a national brand by giving a Chobani yogurt to every person in America.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2018, the company launched Chobani Savor, a brand for their yogurt-based <a href="/wiki/Condiment" title="Condiment">condiment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_responsibility">Community responsibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chobani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Community responsibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chobani_Foundation">Chobani Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chobani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chobani Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the founding of the company, Chobani has donated a portion of profits back into the communities where its employees live and work.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> It’s charitable arm, the Chobani Foundation, was formed in 2010, and focuses on improving childhood nutrition,<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> strengthening the communities near its plants in New York and Idaho, <sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> and supporting those in need after major disasters in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chobani_Incubator">Chobani Incubator</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chobani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chobani Incubator">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hamdi Ulukaya launched the Chobani Food Incubator in 2016 to support food entrepreneurs aiming to challenge the food industry, improve broken systems, and bring better food to more people.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup>  The program provides food and beverage startups with a nonequity investment, resources, mentorship and programming.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, the company announced the creation of the Chobani Incubator Food Tech Residency.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup>
</p><p>Participants in the incubator program include: Banza, Misfit Juicery,<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Chloe's Fruit, Pique Tea Crystals,<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> NOKA,<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> MatchaBar, Ithaca Cold-Crafted, and more.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chobani_shares">Chobani shares</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chobani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chobani shares">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2016, Chobani announced it was giving 10 percent of its ownership stake to its employees.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup>  The company allotted shares to its employees based on tenure, reportedly helping some of the company’s longest serving employees become millionaires.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Paid_parental_leave">Paid parental leave</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chobani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Paid parental leave">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Effective in 2017, Chobani implemented a new paid <a href="/wiki/Parental_leave" title="Parental leave">parental leave</a> policy, offering 100% paid parental leave for six weeks for all full-time hourly and salaried employees that have completed at least 12 months of continuous employment. The program extends to both mothers and fathers for the birth, <a href="/wiki/Adoption" title="Adoption">adoption</a>, or placement of a <a href="/wiki/Foster_care" title="Foster care">foster child</a> into the home.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alex_Jones_lawsuit">Alex Jones lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chobani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Alex Jones lawsuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 24 April 2017, Chobani filed a defamation lawsuit in Idaho state court against <a href="/wiki/Alex_Jones_(radio_host)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alex Jones (radio host)">Alex Jones</a> following a number of allegations made on the <a href="/wiki/InfoWars" title="InfoWars">InfoWars</a> website.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> As a result of the lawsuit, Jones issued an apology and retraction of his allegations on 17 May 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup>
</p>
